Terms and Conditions

I. The Society has Point of Sale devices which can be used to facilitate the payment of goods and services by customers using Zimswitch enabled bank cards. II. The Society wishes to install Point of Sale devices in the Merchant’s premise/s to enable customers who purchase goods and services from the Merchant using bank cards to pay using the Society Point of Sale devices. III. The Merchant is agreeable to the Society installing its Point of Sale devices in its premise/s subject to certain terms and conditions IV. The Parties wish to record the terms and conditions upon which the Society shall install its Point of Sale devices in the Merchant’s premise/s and the terms and conditions upon which the Point of Sale devices shall operate

IT IS AGREED: 1. Installation 1.1 The Society shall install Point of Sale Devices (the “Devices”) in the Merchant’s premises and the number of Devices to be installed in the Merchant’s premises shall be agreed upon in writing between the parties. The Society shall be responsible for installing the Devices and for ensuring that the Devices are properly functional. 1.2 The Devices shall accept ZimSwitch enabled customer debit cards which will enable transactions to be conducted in the applicable currency in Zimbabwe, as may be stipulated by regulatory authorities from time to time. 2. Compliance with Operating Instructions In operating the Society’s POS facilities, the Merchant undertakes: 2.1 to comply with the Society standard operating instructions as may be notified in writing from time to time 2.2 to honour all valid card transactions as authorized by the Society. 2.3 to display all promotional material as may be supplied by the Society from time to time, which indicate that the Merchant accepts payment for goods and services using the Society’s Devices. The Merchant agrees to replace or remove any such promotional material when requested to do so by The Society within 48 hours of receiving the written advice. 3. Customer Cards Accepted 3.1 The Merchant may accept National Building Society and ZimSwitch enabled cards for payments on the POS Devices installed by the Society. The Merchant may not accept any other cards, unless authorized to do so by the Society in writing.

4. Authorizations 4.1. Transaction authorizations shall be PIN based. The Customer’s card shall be swiped through the Device at the point of sale, upon which they will input their specially encoded secret PIN number which will enable authorization of transaction by the Society. 5. Merchant’s obligations 5.1 The Merchant shall keep a copy of all approved transactions print out for a minimum period of six (6) months. 5.2 The Merchant shall be responsible for ensuring that the POS Devices in its premises are not used for unauthorized transactions such as card cloning in good working order and shall report any non-functioning Devices to the Society within three hours of the malfunction.

6. Merchant Settlement. 6.1 The Society will credit the Merchant’s operating account with the Society with the full amount transacted within 24 hours of the transaction. 7. Refund Procedure 7.1 All requests for a refund should be agreed to between the Customer and the Merchant. The Merchant shall stamp and sign the declined slip or write a letter to the Society as an instruction to the Society to debit the Merchant’s account and refund or credit the Customer’s account 7.2 Upon receipt of the stamped copy or letter, the Society shall proceed to refund the Customer within 7 working days. 8. Authority to the Society to debit the Merchant’s Account 8.1 The Merchant agrees that the Society shall recover from the Merchant the following which shall be payable on demand and which shall be debited from the Merchant’s account: 8.1.1 Any refund due to the client in accordance with the refund procedure set out in Clause 7 3 of 4 8.1.2 Overpayment made by the Customer due to clerical errors, whether such errors were made by the Merchant or the Society 8.1.3 Reversal in respect of unsuccessful transactions which go on to debit the Customer’s account.

Notwithstanding the fee structure provided for in clauses 9.1.1 and 9.1.2. above, the Society reserves the right to give notice in writing to The Merchant to vary, change or alter the Merchant Service fees at any time during the subsistence of this agreement. Thereafter, by the continued use of the facility, The Merchant shall be deemed to have agreed to such variation, changes, or alterations.

10. Record of Merchant POS Terminal Transactions 10.1 The Merchant shall maintain receipts and records of all Merchant POS transactions made between Customers and the Merchant for inspection by the Society or Regulatory Authorities from time to time. 10.2 The Society shall maintain records of all Merchant POS transactions for purposes of conducting reconciliations and shall credit the Merchant’s Bank Account at the end of each day with the total value of the Day’s Net Sales or transfer through RTGS the total value of the Day’s Net Sales to the Merchant’s Bankers as indicated in Clause 15.5 11. Duration and Termination 11.1 This agreement supersedes all prior Agreements entered into between the Society and the Merchant in respect of any Devices. 11.2 The agreement shall take effect on the date of signature by the party last to sign; and continue in force until otherwise terminated by the Parties in terms of this agreement. 11.3 Either Party May terminate the Agreement by giving thirty (30) days’ written notice of such termination; or by both Parties agreeing in writing that it should be so terminated. 11.4 Termination of this agreement shall not affect the obligations of either party which arose before the date of termination.

12. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution 12.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with Zimbabwean laws. 12.2 Any difference or dispute arising out of this Agreement shall be resolved through negotiation by the Parties. In the event of no agreement being reached through those negotiations, the dispute shall be referred to arbitration at the instance of either Party. The Arbitrator to preside over the matter shall be appointed by the President (or head by whatever name) for the time being, of the Commercial Arbitration Centre in Harare. 12.3 In the event that the Parties fail to settle and notwithstanding the aforesaid, either Party reserves the right to take legal action before any other competent court in Zimbabwe should it deem it necessary.

13. General 13.1 The head notes to the clause in this Agreement are inserted for reference purposes only and shall not govern or affect its interpretation or construction. 13.2 The Devices supplied by the Society to the Merchant shall at all times remain the property of the Society and upon termination of this agreement the Merchant shall return the terminals to the Society. The Merchant acknowledges that it has a duty of care to the Society in respect of the Devices installed at its premises. 13.3 Where loss or damage to POS Device or its accompanying communication and charging infrastructure is apportioned to the Merchant, repair or replacement costs will be met by the Merchant. 13.4 The Society encourages the Merchant to include under its Insurance Policy, cover for loss through theft or damage to POS Device. 13.5 The Society shall supply and maintain the POS Devices and shall attend to the training of the Merchant’s staff as necessary. 13.6 The Merchant’s rights and duties under this agreement shall not be transferable or assignable. 13.7 The Merchant undertakes to advise the Society in writing of all changes in the Merchant’s banking account arrangements or 4 of 4 of any changes of address. The Society shall not be liable for any loss arising from failure to communicate such change. 13.8 Any relaxation or indulgence or extension of time granted by the Society shall not constitute or be deemed to be a novation or waiver of any of the Society’s rights against the Merchant in terms hereof. 13.9 This Agreement shall constitute the entire agreement and understanding between the parties and shall supersede any previous agreements or arrangements between them governing or concerning the subject matter of this Agreement.

Non-Exclusivity Clause Nothing in this Agreement shall be misconstrued to connote exclusivity between the parties.
